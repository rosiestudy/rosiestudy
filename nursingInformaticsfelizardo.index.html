We're going to learn how to do a proper medical hand washing!

Handwashing is an effective way to prevent the spread of bacterial and viral illnesses.

Step-by-step guide to wash the hands, follow the simple steps in the diagram above. For a more in-depth guide to hand washing, follow these steps:
1. Wet your hands with plenty of clean water.
2. Cover all the surfaces of your hands in soap.
3. Rub the palms together to form a lather.
4. Rub the palm of one hand over the back of the other hand, making sure to clean in between your fingers. Repeat with the other hand.
5. Rub the palms together again, and also clean in between the fingers again.
6. Rub the backs of the fingers against the opposite palm, interlocking the fingers as you do this.
7. Grasp the thumb of one hand with the other hand, and rotate the closed hand around the thumb to clean it. Repeat with the other thumb and hand.
8. Rub the tips of the fingers of one hand on the palm of the other hand. Repeat with the other hand.
9. If a clean nail brush is available, scrub gently under the nails.
10. Rinse the hands under clean, running water. 11. Dry them thoroughly, ideally, with a disposable towel. Alternatively, allow them to air dry.
12. Use the towel (if you have one) to turn off the tap and then dispose of it.
